How It Began

My mountain biking journey began only a few months ago. In February, I began training three days a week riding the Pipeline Trail and Sammamish River Trail in Woodinville, Washington to get in good enough shape to try mountain biking for the first time. I borrowed my aunts hybrid bike and decided to give the Pipeline hill a shot for the first time. I had no idea what I had gotten myself into; the Pipeline hill is huge and very steep. I was only able to pedal about 15 feet up the hill and had push the bike up the rest of the way. It took at least 20 minutes and a great deal of mental effort to push my bike up the hill while my calves burned, sweat dripped into my eyes and I gasped for air. Even runners passing me were offering words of encouragement and sympathetic looks. Over an hour later, I finally made it home and honestly wasn't sure if I was going to have the courage to ride the pipeline trail again. A few days later I got back on my borrowed bike that was now covered in mud and pedalled up the hill just a little bit farther than the frist time. I continued training in the rain and even the snow, reminding myself that I only needed to make it just a little bit farther than the last time. After 6 weeks of riding/walking up the Pipeline hill, I made it to the top, collapsed on the ground and decided to buy a mountain bike. If I could do that, I could do anything. Plus after all that hard work, I totally deserved it. Since purchasing my mountain bike in the middle of March I have been mountain biking at Duthie Hill, Black Diamond and even the grueling and challening 12 mile loop at Tiger Mountain. I work in the afternoon/evening and like to go mountain biking on the weekdays before work since the trails are usually quiet and I can use the skill building area at Duthie Hill.
